VM creation on GCP

Step 1

Go to “https://cloud.google.com/” and click “Console”

Step 2

You will be asked to agree to the Terms of Service, Agree and continue and you should see this screen. Click on “Select a Project”.

Step 3

On the pop up window select the organization for work or university from the dropdown and then click “NEW PROJECT"

Step 4

On the following screen then choose a Project Name, Organization and/or Location and click “CREATE”

Step 5

Go back to the console and from “Select a Project” choose the project name you have created in the previous step. From the menu on the left, select “Billing”, then “LINK A BILLING ACCOUNT” and set an account

Step 6

From the console menu, go to “Compute Engine” and click VM instances

Step 7

Once the Compute engine is ready, click “Create”

Step 8

Now, choose the configuration for your VM which will also decide the pricing estimate. You can choose a name, the region in which the VM will be located based on your needs, machine series (Check Google’s documentation for this. Different series support different configurations. Example – Only selected series support a GPU)

Step 9

You can also create a custom Machine Type by selecting the number of CPUs and RAM required. (The screenshots are from the time and day I was creating this, the pricing and other details can change based on various factors)

Step 10

You can also select the CPU platform and add GPUs. From the Boot Disk -> “Change” option, select the OS and disk size

Step 11

You can set Firewall rules as per your requirements. After, you have set your desired VM configuration, it’s time to “Create”. (You may want to check your pricing estimate before this)

Step 12

Your VM instance name will appear on the screen and the green check indicates it has been created successfully. You can now set a password and use the “External IP” to remotely connect and use the VM
